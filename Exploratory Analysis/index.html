Introduction

The western United States has faced increasing amounts of wildfires in the last decade. Wildfires are a major threat to the population’s own health and the health of our environment. Currently, many speculate that the increasing amounts of wildfires may be due to climate change and it’s associated increasing temperatures. To understand more about wildfires in the West coast, our report investigates the trends in wildfires, specifically in California.

Summary

In order to analyze the trends within the last decade, our analysis focuses on the years 2013 to 2020. Wildfires show tremendous consequences on our environment as each year, 1,070,651 acres of land were impacted by wildfires. Specifically, Riverside county suffered from wildfires the most during this time period. Additionally, 2017 was the worst year for wildfires in California, since the most occurred during that year. We also wanted to look at the proportion of equipment used by firefighters and personnel, and found that engines are used the most. The proportions of these equipment used are helicopters, dozers, and engines and their proportions are, respectively, 0.017, 0.075, and 0.117. Lastly, we were curious about the average labor participated in handling the wildfires, which were about 580 in 2013 and decreased to 227 in 2017, with the average change of 60%.
